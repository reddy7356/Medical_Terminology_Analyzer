Discharge Instructions:
Call Your Neurosurgeon If You Experience:
- Any neurological issues, such as changes in vision, speech, or 
movement.
- Redness, swelling, or drainage from your surgical incision.
- Any problems with medications, such as lethargy, nausea, or 
vomiting.
- Fever greater than 101.5 degrees Fahrenheit.
- Headaches not relieved with prescribed medications.

Activity:
- Start to resume all activities as you tolerate, but start 
slowly and increase at your own pace.
- Do not operate any motorized vehicle after surgery, you can 
get more details about this at the time of your suture removal.

Medications: 
- Please do not take your birth control for one week after 
surgery. You may resume it on ___. Please follow-up with 
your primary care provider with any questions or concerns.

Surgical Incision Care:
- Keep your surgical incision clean and dry.
- Do not use shampoo until your sutures are removed. 
- When you are allowed to shampoo your hair, let the shampoo run 
off the surgical incision line. Gently pad the surgical incision 
with a towel to dry.
- Do not rub, scrub, scratch, or pick at any scabs on the 
surgical incision line.
- You will need your sutures removed ___ days after surgery.

Postoperative Experiences: Physical
- You may experience jaw pain on the same side as your surgery, 
this goes away after about a month.
- You may experience constipation. Constipation can be prevented 
by:
  - Drinking plenty of fluids.
  - Increasing fiber in your diet by eating prunes, vegetables, 
fiber rich breads or cereals, and fiber supplements.
  - Exercising.
  - Using over the counter laxatives or bowel stimulants as 
needed. Please stop taking if you experience loose stools or 
diarrhea.
- Fatigue, which will slowly resolve over time.
- Numbness or tingling in the area of the surgical incision. 
This can take weeks or months to fully resolve.
- Muffled hearing in the ear near the surgical incision.
- Low back pain or shooting pains down the legs, which should 
resolve with increased activity.

Postoperative Experiences: Emotional
- You may experience depression. Symptoms of depression can 
include:
  - Feeling “down” or sad.
  - Irritability, frustration, and confusion.
  - Distractibility.
  - Lower self esteem.
  - Relationship challenges.
  - Insomnia.
  - Loneliness.
- If you experience these symptoms, please contact your primary 
care provider, who can make a referral to a psychologist or 
psychiatrist.
